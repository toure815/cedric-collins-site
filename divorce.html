Divorce or dissolution? Both end a marriage.

i.

Dissolution

A no-fault, mutual end to a marriage. Both spouses agree on all terms first, then file together. Often faster and less costly.

ii.

Uncontested divorce

You agree on most issues but need the court to formalize the rest. We keep it efficient and protect your interests.

iii.

Contested divorce

When agreement is not possible, you need a steady advocate who will fight for a fair outcome in court.

FAQ

Common divorce questions.

What is the difference between divorce and dissolution in Ohio?+

A dissolution is a no-fault, mutual end to a marriage where both spouses agree on all terms before filing. A divorce is a court action used when spouses cannot agree, and the court resolves the contested issues.

How is property divided in an Ohio divorce?+

Ohio is an equitable distribution state, which means marital property is divided fairly, though not always exactly equally. Separate property generally stays with the spouse who owns it.

Do I need a lawyer for an uncontested divorce?+

Even when both spouses agree, a lawyer makes sure the paperwork is correct and your rights are protected so the agreement holds up over time. A free consultation will tell you exactly what your situation needs.

How long does a divorce take in Ohio?+

An uncontested dissolution can often be finalized within a few months. A contested divorce involving custody or property disputes takes longer. We give you a realistic timeline at your consultation.
