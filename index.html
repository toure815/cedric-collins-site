Questions families ask before they call.

Do you offer a free consultation for divorce cases?+

Yes. Your first conversation is free and completely confidential. You will leave knowing where you stand and what your options are, whether or not you decide to work together.

What areas of Ohio do you serve?+

The office is in Pickerington and serves the greater Columbus area, including Franklin, Fairfield, and Licking Counties, where attorney Collins is also appointed by the courts as a Guardian ad Litem.

How much does a divorce attorney cost in Columbus, Ohio?+

Every divorce is different, and cost depends on whether your case is contested and how complex the issues are. Your first consultation is free, and you will get an honest picture of likely costs before you commit to anything.

How long does a divorce take in Ohio?+

An uncontested dissolution can often be finalized within a few months. A contested divorce involving custody or property disputes takes longer. We give you a realistic timeline for your specific situation at your consultation.

Do you handle child custody and parenting time?+

Yes. Custody, parenting time, and decisions in your children’s best interest are a core focus of the practice. Attorney Collins also serves as a court-appointed Guardian ad Litem.
