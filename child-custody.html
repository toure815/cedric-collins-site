FAQ

Common custody questions.

How does an Ohio court decide custody?+

Ohio courts decide custody based on the best interest of the child, weighing factors such as each parent’s relationship with the child, the child’s needs, stability, and the ability of the parents to cooperate.

What is a Guardian ad Litem?+

A Guardian ad Litem is a person the court appoints to investigate and represent the best interest of a child in a custody or juvenile case. Attorney Collins serves in this role across three Central Ohio counties, which gives him real insight into how these decisions are made.

Can a custody or parenting time order be changed?+

Yes. When circumstances change significantly, a court can modify custody or parenting time if the change serves the child’s best interest. We can review whether your situation qualifies.
